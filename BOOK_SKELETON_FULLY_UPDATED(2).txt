    2. Bomb #2: Synthetic Leverage (CDOs, CDS, SIVs)

        ◦ Real: same cash flows get sliced, diced, re-insured, and rehypothecated into infinite “products.”

        ◦ Alt:

            ▪ derivatives are only allowed for plain-vanilla hedging;

            ▪ complex synthetic structures must be registered as public utilities or are banned.

        ◦ Speculative leverage over a certain multiple is treated like illegal dumping.

    3. Bomb #3: Shadow Banking & Off-Balance-Sheet Vehicles

        ◦ Real: securitization conduits, SIVs, repo chains — all invisible until they freeze.

        ◦ Alt: shadow banking simply doesn’t get legal oxygen.

            ▪ All credit intermediaries must be chartered, fully consolidated on transparent balance sheets, and stress-tested in public.

    4. Bomb #4: Rating Agency Capture & Regulatory Arbitrage

        ◦ Real: agencies get paid by issuers, bless trash as AAA, regulators outsource judgment.

        ◦ Alt:

            ▪ ratings are provided by public or cooperative bodies with strict conflict-of-interest rules, or risk models are run directly by regulators.

            ▪ banks are supervised on underlying loan quality, not just external ratings.

Then you do the counterfactual sequence:
    • 2003–2006 in real world = bubble inflation years.

    • In the alternate world:

        ◦ housing does get more affordable via public build-to-rent and co-ops, not via junk credit.

        ◦ when regional prices start running hot, automatic loan-to-value brakes kick in.

        ◦ regulators literally cannot ignore it; public dashboards show when housing diverges from income.

 It doesn’t happen because the architecture doesn’t allow it.
